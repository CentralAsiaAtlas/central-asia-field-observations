# Mongolia Field Observations (2017)

## Context

Independent field visit to Altanbulag District, Töv Province, Mongolia, including time living with a nomadic herding family.

The observations recorded here are adapted from notes written during the visit in 2017. They have been reorganized as field 
observations that later inspired broader questions about rural livelihoods, agriculture, environmental change, and 
international development.

## Field Observation 01: Curiosity Preceded Research

This visit was not undertaken as a research project.

My interest in Central Asia developed gradually over many years through maps, history, geography, and conversations with people from the region. After learning about opportunities to live with nomadic families in Mongolia, I chose to participate because I wanted to experience everyday rural life rather than visit as a tourist.

At the time, I was working as an engineer and had no plans to pursue independent research in rural development. Looking back, however, many of the questions that now motivate my work first emerged during this experience.

### Reflection

I did not arrive in Mongolia looking for research questions.

The questions emerged naturally from living alongside a family whose daily decisions were shaped by livestock, weather, education, tradition, and limited infrastructure.

That experience changed the kinds of problems I became interested in understanding.
